Scratch Programming is a block-based visual programming language targeted specially for younger children. Scratchers can create their own program through block-like interface.

Scratch got its name from a technique that ‘disc jockeys’ use - “Scratching” where different sound effects and music is produced. Scratchers can mix different media together in creative ways or remixing projects like video games, animations and simulations.

Scratch programming encourages creative thinking and collaboration amongst users, which are critical soft skillsets to inculcate from a young age.

It enables students to express their own ideas, and learning different computational thinking skills from thousands of different coders. They will develop the the ability to carry out their ideas from start to finish, and even problem solving skills. Little do people know that many important life skills can be learnt from programming.

Game development is the process of developing a game, either singlehandedly, or with a group of developers. The game industry has been on the rise with the growth of accessible development software.
Game development comes in a few parts, a good story, design, sound, programming, development and the release itself.
Might not seem easy to learn, but it is not difficult to understand as well.

Game development allows programmers to learn programming and coding better, by understanding algorithms, data structure and performance optimization, at a young age.
Children have the most imaginative brains and learning game development can enhance their imagination skills and the ability to produce new things. Game development triggers thinking about various aspects like game mechanics, rewards, player engagement and level design, allowing development of the child’s brain and critical thinking abilities.

Young computer scientist marries the hardware (mBot) and software (coding), allowing students to be mathematicians and scientists. The nature of this course is an experimentation in the STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics) field with endless possibilities waiting to be discovered.
We have 3 different research areas to expose students to different areas of application of coding (Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Augmented Reality).
* THIS COURSE REQUIRES A PLACEMENT TEST

Young Computer Scientists, unlike the other 2 programmes, allows students to focus on 1 research area that they interest them and focus more on applying that function into their daily lives.
This programme requires a deeperunderstanding of coding and development, unlike the other 2, hence there is a need for a placement test to better understand our student’s pace of learning and understanding before starting the class.
